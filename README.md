# Global Graduate Placement & Compensation Analysis (Data Challenge)

## 📌 Project Overview
This data analytics project evaluates the multi-dimensional factors that influence job placement outcomes and starting salaries for graduates globally. Utilizing a dataset of 10,000 records across five major economies (USA, UK, Canada, Germany, and India), the analysis uncovers how educational prestige, industry choice, academic performance, and macroeconomic location intersect to dictate entry-level career trajectories.

## 📊 Key Insights & Analytical Dimensions

### 1. The Industry Impact (Compensation Structures)
An analysis of placed candidates reveals that industry selection heavily weights initial earning potential. 
* **Top Earners:** The **Tech** (~$93,051) and **Finance** (~$92,542) sectors command the highest average starting salaries.
* **Stable Baselines:** Healthcare and Manufacturing maintain strong baselines, averaging roughly $89,414 and $88,742 respectively.

### 2. Educational Tier Dynamics & Geographic Premiums
The data confirms a stark premium for Tier 1 institutions, though the economic return varies substantially by nation:
* In the **USA**, Tier 1 graduates average **$119,726** compared to **$97,046** for Tier 3 graduates.
* **India** displays a distinct localized compensation structure, with Tier 1 salaries averaging ~$60,069 and Tier 3 at ~$41,787.
* Across all countries, graduating from a Tier 1 institution provides a definitive competitive advantage in starting salary.

### 3. Quantitative Performance Benchmarks (Placement Realities)
Evaluating academic and assessment data reveals clear differentiators between placed and non-placed candidates:
* **Academic Baseline:** Placed candidates maintained a higher average CGPA (**7.21**) compared to non-placed individuals (**6.66**).
* **Technical Aptitude:** Placed individuals performed consistently better on standardized assessment metrics, averaging a score of **137.21** vs. **131.56** for their non-placed peers.

### 4. Macroeconomic Placement Distributions
The distribution of job placements remains remarkably balanced across the tracked regions, with each nation processing ~2,000 candidate lifecycles:
* Total Placed: **6,153** candidates
* Total Not Placed: **3,847** candidates
* The USA and India led slightly in net positive placement volumes, demonstrating robust hiring market conditions.
